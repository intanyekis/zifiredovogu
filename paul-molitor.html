    Biography

    Paul Molitor was born in Saint Paul, MN on August 22, 1956. Hall of Fame infielder and 7-time MLB All-Star who retired with a career batting average of .306. He spent the first 15 years of his career with the Milwaukee Brewers, then spent three years apiece with the Toronto Blue Jays and Minnesota Twins. His thirty-nine-game hitting streak fell just short of the longest post-World War II hitting streak, which was set by Pete Rose. He was selected in the twenty-eighth round of the 1974 MLB Draft as a pitcher.
