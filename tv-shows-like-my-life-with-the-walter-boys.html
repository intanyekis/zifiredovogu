    My Life with the Walter Boys is coming-of-age drama is an adaptation of Ali Novak’s 2014 novel of the same name. We have officially discovered your new TV passion, adolescent drama aficionados. Recently, the Netflix original tv show ‘My Life With The Walter Boys’ tells the story of Jackie (Nikki Rodriguez), who leaves her life in Manhattan after a tragic tragedy leaves her an orphan and relocates to rural Colorado to live with her guardians, the Walter family and their several children.

    The teenage years is undoubtedly a time of great drama. These fascinating TV episodes show how it also fulfils romance, humour and a healthy dose of mystery. Here’s everything you need to know about 15 TV shows like ‘My Life with the Walter Boys’ which can help you spent your weekend curled up on the couch binge-watching the show and can’t get enough.

    1. My First First Love (2019)

    My First First Love (Korean: 첫사랑은 처음이라서) is a South Korean tv show tells the story of five youths and their messy encounters with the concept of first love. Yun Tae-o is a college student whose friends — a college drop-out, a runaway family friend, and his long-time childhood friend — decide to unexpectedly move into his house, due to their own individual reasonings. Now they must all learn to live together and learn to love.

    2. DI4RIES

    Di4ries was first released by Netflix in Italy on 18 May 2022 and a coming-of-age television series. In the first and second seasons of the show, a group of kids from Galileo Galilei Middle School are followed as they attend the made-up island cities of Marina Grande and Marina Piccola.

    The youngsters negotiate puberty, crushes, rivalries, and friendships during the course of the series. A different figure serves as the narrator and frequently breaks the fourth wall in each episode. Show stars Andrea Arru as Pietro Maggi, Flavia Leone as Livia Mancini and Sofia Nicolini as Isabel Diop.

    3. Never Have I Ever (2020)

    Never Have I Ever is about an Indian-American high school student dealing with the sudden death of her father and the bumpy journey. The tv show has total number of 4 seasons which casts, Maitreyi Ramakrishnan as Devi Vishwakumar, Poorna Jagannathan as Dr. Nalini Vishwakumar, Richa Moorjani as Kamala Nandiwadal and Jaren Lewison as Benjamin “Ben” Gross.

    4. XO, Kitty (2023)

    Teen matchmaker Kitty, who attended the same boarding school as her late mother, reunites with her long-distance boyfriend, setting off a new love story. The show casts Anna Cathcart as Katherine “Kitty” Song Covey, Choi Min-young as Dae-heon Kim and Anthony Keyvan as Quincy “Q” Shabazian.

    5. Everything Now (2023)

    The story follows Mia Polanco as she returns to sixth form after a long battle with anorexia and is released from the hospital. The series created by Ripley Parker, starring Sophie Wilde. It casts Sophie Wilde as Mia Polanco, Lauryn Ajufo as Becca Lloyd, Harry Cadby as Cameron “Cam”, Noah Thomas as William “Will” and Sam Reuben as Alex, Mia’s brother.

    6. 13 Reasons Why

    13 Reasons Why is an another American teen drama which revolves around high school student Clay Jensen (Dylan Minnette) and the aftermath of the suicide of fellow student Hannah Baker (Katherine Langford).

    Through its various storylines, the show explores and depicts a wide range of social issues affecting modern youth. The show casts, Dylan Minnette as Clay Jensen, Katherine Langford as Hannah Baker, Christian Navarro as Tony Padilla, Alisha Boe as Jessica Davis and Brandon Flynn as Justin Foley.

    7. On My Block

    The gritty inner-city Los Angeles neighbourhood of Freeridge serves as the setting for the fictional television series ‘On My Block’, which centres on the lives of four streetwise friends as they navigate high school while juggling new crushes, gang activity, and other normal teenage issues.

    The four friends—Monse, Ruby, Cesar, and Jamal—are referred to as the Core Four. It casts Sierra Capri as Monsé Finnie, Jason Genao as Ruben “Ruby” Martinez Jr., Brett Gray as Jamal Turner, Jessica Marie Garcia as Jasmine Flores, Julio Macias as Oscar “Spooky” Diaz, Peggy Blow as Marisol Martinez ‘Abuelita’ and Diego Tinoco as Cesar Diaz.

    8. Heartstopper

    Heartstopper is a British coming-of-age romantic comedy-drama television series. It is available to stream on Netflix. The main cast of the series is Joe Locke‘s Charlie Spring, a gay schoolboy who develops feelings for his fellow student Nick Nelson (Kit Connor), with whom he shares a seat in his transformed form.

    The lives of their pals Elle Argent (Yasmin Finney), Tao Xu (William Gao), Tara Jones (Corinna Brown), Isaac Henderson (Tobie Donovan), and Darcy Olsson (Kizzy Edgell) are also explored.

    9. Gossip Girl

    Gossip Girl is another masterpiece that revolves around the lives of privileged upper-class adolescents living in Manhattan’s Upper East Side (UES). Main characters include Blake Lively, Leighton Meester, Penn Badgley, Chace Crawford and Taylor Momsen.

    10. Greenhouse Academy

    Greenhouse Academy is a tween drama television series released by Netflix and the fourth season was released on March 20, 2020. Movie synopsis read as, “When teen siblings Hayley and Alex enter an elite boarding school, they find rivalry, romance and a mystery related to the recent loss of their mom.” Starring Ariel Mortman, Finn Roberts and Chris O’Neal.

    11. Riverdale

    This tv series come in 2017 that revolves around the troubled waters of sex, romance, school and family, teen Archie and his gang become entangled in a dark Riverdale mystery. Lead in the show are K.J. Apa, Lili Reinhart and Camila Mendes.

    12. The Rain

    The Rain storyline began on the note of, ‘Six years after a rain-borne virus wipes out most of Scandinavia’s population, two siblings join a band of young survivors seeking safety — and answers.’ It stars Alba August, Lucas Lynggaard Tønnesen and Mikkel Boe Følsgaard.
