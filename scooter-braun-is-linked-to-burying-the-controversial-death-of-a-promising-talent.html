    Scooter Braun's Business Partner Kept His Young Talent's Death A Secret

    On November 8, 2023, Business Insider released an exposé on Bolno sweeping Sterling's death under the rug. The report revealed that on March 30 this year, the 24-year-old talent "took the elevator up to Room 2805 of the Setai Hotel's VIP Ocean Suites [in Miami] ... to visit David Bolno, Scooter Braun's business partner and the money manager for stars like Drake and Post Malone."

    Around 4:30 a.m., Bolno went out and left Sterling in the room. She was found dead in the bathtub about eight hours later when the concierge noticed that no one had checked out. In the photograph of the scene, there were "tusi, a drug cocktail containing MDMA and ketamine" and "a condom wrapper in the garbage." Official reports say that Sterling had died of accidental overdose.

    Bolno was initially a person of interest until he cooperated with the Miami Beach Police Department. When asked why he was with Sterling at the hotel, the music executive said: "She was recording music the night before. She's a client and she was going to sign a record deal with me. I helped her make music. There's not much more to it."

    His statement proved to be a lie when text messages between him and his talent revealed that he paid rent on her condo. She also referred to him as her "boyfriend," and her friends said that their affair was an "open secret." Bolno, who's married with two children, has been Braun's right-hand man for 17 years.

    "I think he has always been Scooter's executioner. He's the bad guy when he needs to be, and I think that allows Scooter to sort of play the good guy," said a manager who's previously worked with the duo. "Bolno doesn't really do anything that Scooter hasn't already sanctioned or approved from a business point of view."

    Bolno has effectively detached his name from the case. However, Business Insider wrote that within the upper industry crowd, "everyone knows" what really happened that night. Someone who was doing a business deal with him and Braun noted that the pair had "ghosted" him after Sterling's passing.

    David Bolno was the chief operating officer of Scooter Braun's Ithaca Holdings. He was also "hands-on" during the infamous acquisition of Taylor Swift's masters.

I read that and was like wtf does he think this makes them look good?"

    Why Celebrities Are Leaving Scooter Braun's Management

    In August 2023, Braun's precious stars began to cut ties with him, one by one. Aside from Bieber, Grande, and Lovato, other singers have also fired him, like Broadway fixture and Glee alum Idina Menzel, Colombian "Prince of Reggaeton" J Balvin, Carly Rae Jepsen, BabyJake, and Asher Roth. According to Vox, "This could all just be about K-Pop."

    In 2021, South Korean studio Hybe, which is responsible for BTS' stardom, acquired Braun's company, Ithaca Holdings. The "billion-dollar merger" is speculated to be keeping the exec too busy to handle his former clients. "Scooter’s team at SB Projects are still handling both Justin and Ariana as they work through what this new structure looks like," a source told the outlet in August 2023.
