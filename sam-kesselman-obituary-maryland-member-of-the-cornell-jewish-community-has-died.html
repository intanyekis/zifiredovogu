    Sam Kesselman Obituary, Death – Sam’s impact on our community and beyond cannot be overstated. In his brief time with us, he left an indelible mark, embodying qualities that serve as a compass for our collective future. As a student, Sam’s leadership shone through in various roles. His presidency at the Zeta Beta Tau chapter at Cornell showcased his ability to inspire and unite. Simultaneously, as a teaching assistant in public policy, he demonstrated an exemplary commitment to education and mentorship.

    His role as a new-member educator for Alpha Kappa Psi underscored his dedication to fostering growth and leadership in others. Throughout these endeavors, he led not only with authority but, more importantly, through the power of his own example. Beyond academia, Sam’s civic engagement reflected a deep commitment to improving the world. His involvement in Jake Auchincloss’ congressional campaign highlighted a passion for meaningful change.

    His contributions extended beyond politics, actively participating in initiatives aimed at enhancing the well-being of his community. For over a decade, he played an integral role in the Camp Modin community, leaving an enduring impact as a camper, counselor, and mentor. Within the family, Sam served as the unifying force, especially as the “glue” among siblings Ross and Rose. His approach was characterized by love, humor, and unwavering generosity. As parents, we were privileged to have him as our son, a source of immeasurable love and respect.

    In his personal life, Sam’s relationship with Irene reflected a profound connection. For nearly five years, their bond epitomized love, kindness, and unwavering support. Their shared dreams painted a picture of a future filled with promise and shared joy. Sam’s legacy is a testament to the positive impact one individual can have on the world. His service, integrity, and empathy serve as a guiding light for us all. Though his time with us was tragically short, the imprint he left on our hearts and the world at large will endure, inspiring generations to come.
