(1984), Khandhar (1984), Khamosh (1985), Raakh (1989), Roja (1992), Dus (2005), Matru Ki Bijlee Ka Mandola (2013), etc. Moreover, he is also renowned for his work in the television series Office Office (2001-2006) and Karamchand (1985-1988; 2007).

    Pankaj Kapur Facts

  • His first major film role was playing Mahatma Gandhi’s second secretary, Pyarelal Nayyar in Richard Attenborough‘s Oscar-winning film, Gandhi (1982). Furthermore, he even dubbed for Ben Kingsley‘s titular role in the Hindi version of the movie.
  • Pankaj shared screen space with his son for the first time in the sports drama, Jersey (2022).
  • He made his film directorial debut with Mausam (2011) starring Shahid and Sonam Kapoor in lead roles. However, the film failed to do well at the box office.
  • In 1993, Pankaj starred as the Hindi teacher Mohan Bharti in the sitcom, Zabaan Sambhalke. It was a Hindi remake of the 1970s British sitcom, Mind Your Language wherein the actor Barry Evans essayed the character of the English teacher named Jeremy Brown. 
  • His captivating performance as the antagonist Abbaji in Vishal Bharadwaj‘s Maqbool (2003) (based on Shakespeare’s Macbeth) earned him the National Film Award for ‘Best Supporting Actor’ in 2004.
  • Pankaj has cited Dilip Kumar, Amitabh Bachchan, Marlon Brando, Robert De Niro, and Dustin Hoffman as his acting role models.
  • In November 2019, he published his debut literary work, a novella titled Dopehri that he had originally penned in 1992.
